Understanding Structural Intelligence

By Dr. Helena Vost and Marcus Thorne
share Interactive Graph

01. Abstract

In the landscape of emerging technologies, structural intelligence defines the nexus between physical form and cognitive processing. This paper argues that the next evolution of AI is not purely algorithmic but deeply integrated into the geometric constraints of its hosting environment.

We examine how entropy, traditionally viewed as a degradation of order, acts as a primary catalyst for semantic discovery within unstructured data lakes. By leveraging asymmetric grid systems, we can visualize the "gravity" of information—where data density creates natural focal points for automated reasoning.
