Structural Neural Synchrony: Phasic Alignment in Non-Linear Cortical Networks

Author

Dr. K. Sakane

The emergence of synchronous firing patterns across spatially disparate neural populations remains one of the most enigmatic phenomena in modern neuroscience. By investigating the structural constraints that facilitate these alignments, we can begin to map the invisible architecture of thought itself. Neural synchrony is not merely a byproduct of communication, but the medium through which the brain achieves coherent global states from localized chaos.
